Safety Measures While Traveling on a Bus

  • Wear a Mask: Always wear a mask that covers your nose and mouth during the entire journey.
  • Practice Hand Hygiene: Carry a hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol content and use it frequently.
  • Maintain Social Distancing: Try to maintain a safe distance from other passengers while boarding, exiting, or waiting for the bus.
  • Ventilation: If possible, sit near open windows to improve ventilation inside the bus.
  • Avoid Crowded Buses: Choose less crowded buses or travel during off-peak hours to minimize close contact with others.
  • Avoid Unnecessary Touching: Minimize contact with surfaces and avoid touching handrails, door handles, and other shared surfaces if not necessary.
  • Cover Coughs and Sneezes: Always cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your elbow when coughing or sneezing.
  • Stay Home if Unwell: If you feel unwell or show any symptoms of illness, avoid traveling on the bus to protect others.
  • Follow Bus Operator's Guidelines: Adhere to any specific safety guidelines issued by the bus operator.
  • Use Contactless Payment: If possible, use contactless payment methods to avoid handling cash.
  • Keep Personal Belongings Clean: Regularly clean and disinfect your personal belongings like bags and mobile phones.
  • Avoid Eating or Drinking: Refrain from eating or drinking on the bus to reduce the need to remove your mask.
  • Be Patient and Cooperative: Follow the instructions of the bus driver and staff, and be patient with any additional safety measures implemented.
